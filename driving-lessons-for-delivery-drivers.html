Skills for Delivery Driving

Our delivery-focused lessons go beyond basic test preparation to teach the practical skills that professional delivery work demands across Manchester.

Route Navigation
Master efficient route planning across Manchester’s road network. Learn to use sat-nav effectively while maintaining road awareness—a skill delivery drivers use every working day.
Loading Bay Manoeuvres
Practise reversing into tight spaces, parallel parking on busy streets, and positioning in loading bays. These are the manoeuvres delivery drivers perform dozens of times daily.
Time Management
Learn to drive efficiently without compromising safety. Delivery work requires good time awareness, and we teach you to maintain progress while staying within speed limits and driving defensively.
Urban Hazard Awareness
Manchester’s residential areas present unique hazards: children, pets, cyclists, and pedestrians between parked cars. Delivery drivers encounter these constantly, so we train awareness intensively.
Career Guide

Your Path to a Delivery Career

Step 1: Get Your Driving Licence

The first step is obtaining your Category B driving licence through professional tuition. Our structured programme covers every DVSA requirement while incorporating the practical skills delivery work demands. Most students are test-ready within 30 to 40 hours of professional instruction.

Step 2: Build Experience

After passing, spend a few weeks driving your own car to build familiarity and confidence with independent driving. Manchester’s diverse road network—from tight terraced streets in Salford to dual carriageways on the A56—provides natural practice for the conditions delivery drivers face daily.

Step 3: Apply to Delivery Companies

Major delivery companies in Manchester including Amazon, DPD, Evri, and Royal Mail regularly recruit drivers. Most require a full driving licence held for at least six months, a clean licence, and the right to work in the UK. Some platforms like Amazon Flex allow you to start using your own vehicle.

Career Boost: The UK delivery sector is growing rapidly with e-commerce. Qualified delivery drivers in Manchester earn £12–18/hr depending on the company and route.
