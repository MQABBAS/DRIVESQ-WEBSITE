MSPSL Explained

What is MSPSL?

Mirror Signal Position Speed Look (MSPSL) — The MSPSL routine is the foundation of safe driving. It stands for Mirror, Signal, Position, Speed, Look — and must be applied at every junction, roundabout, and hazard.

MSPSL is applied to: junctions, roundabouts, lane changes, overtaking, pulling over, moving off. In Clayton, you will use this technique dozens of times on every lesson.

Common MSPSL Mistakes

Checking mirrors but not acting on what you see
Signalling before checking mirrors
Positioning too late — you should be in position BEFORE slowing down
Not adjusting speed enough for the hazard
Only looking once instead of continuously assessing
Examiner Insight

Examiners mark mirror checks as a serious fault if you don't check mirrors before signalling or changing direction. They're watching your eyes — quick glances don't count.
