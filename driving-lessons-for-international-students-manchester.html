Licence Guide

International Licence Rules in the UK

Driving on Your Foreign Licence

If you hold a valid driving licence from a designated country (including EU nations, Australia, Canada, and others), you can drive in the UK for up to 12 months from the date you became resident. After 12 months, you must either exchange your licence or pass the UK driving test to continue driving legally.

Non-Designated Country Licences

If your licence is from a non-designated country (including most of Asia, Africa, and South America), you can drive on your foreign licence for 12 months but cannot exchange it. You must pass both the UK theory test and practical test to obtain a full UK licence. DriveSQ can prepare you for both tests with focused instruction.

Theory Test for International Learners

The UK theory test is available in English only. It covers 50 multiple-choice questions on the Highway Code and 14 hazard perception video clips.
