Privacy Policy

How DriveSQ Driving School Manchester collects, uses, and protects your personal data. Last updated: June 2025.

1. Who We Are

DriveSQ Driving School Manchester (“we”, “us”, “our”) is a DVSA-approved driving school operating across Greater Manchester. We are the data controller for the personal information we collect from you. You can contact us at any time via WhatsApp on 07352 932003.

2. What Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following personal data:

  • Name — your full name as provided when booking lessons or making enquiries.
  • Phone number — used for lesson scheduling and WhatsApp communication.
  • Email address — if provided during booking or enquiry.
  • Lesson booking details — preferred dates, times, lesson type (manual or automatic), pick-up location, and learning stage.
  • Technical data — IP address, browser type, device type, and browsing actions on our website (collected automatically via cookies and analytics).

3. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Booking management — to schedule, confirm, reschedule, and manage your driving lessons.
  • Communication via WhatsApp — to respond to enquiries, send booking confirmations, lesson reminders, and coordinate with you about your lessons.
  • Lesson scheduling — to coordinate instructor availability, pick-up locations, and lesson plans.
  • Service improvement — to analyse website usage through Google Analytics and improve our services.
  • Legal compliance — to meet our obligations under UK law and DVSA requirements.

We will never sell your personal data to third parties. We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling.

4. Cookies and Google Analytics

Our website uses cookies to ensure proper functionality and to collect anonymous usage statistics. Specifically, we use:

  • Google Analytics (G-CQP798G5TW) — We use Google Analytics 4 to collect anonymised data about how visitors use our website, including pages visited, time spent on pages, referral sources, and device information. Google Analytics uses cookies (such as _ga and _ga_*) to distinguish unique users. This data is aggregated and does not personally identify you.
  • Essential cookies — required for basic website functionality such as navigation.

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when a cookie is being set. For more information about Google Analytics and how to opt out, visit the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

5. Third-Party Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data to any third party. We may share your data with the following parties only when necessary:

  • Google Analytics (Google LLC) — anonymised website usage data as described in Section 4. See Google’s Privacy Policy.
  • WhatsApp / Meta Platforms, Inc. — when you contact us via WhatsApp, your messages are processed by WhatsApp in accordance with WhatsApp’s Privacy Policy.
  • Legal authorities — if required by law, regulation, or legal proceedings.

6. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you can ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — you can request that we delete your personal data, subject to legal and regulatory obligations.
  • Right to data portability — you can request your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — you can object to our processing of your data where we rely on legitimate interest as our lawful basis.
  • Right to restrict processing — you can ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us via WhatsApp on 07352 932003 or call us on 07352 932003. We will respond to your request within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO).

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Booking and lesson records — retained for up to 3 years after your last lesson to handle any follow-up enquiries or disputes.
  • Communication records — retained for up to 2 years after your last interaction with us.
  • Website analytics data — Google Analytics data is retained for 14 months (the default GA4 retention period).

After the retention period, your data is securely deleted or anonymised.

8. Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or alteration. This includes using encrypted communications (WhatsApp end-to-end encryption, HTTPS on our website) and limiting access to personal data to only those who need it.

9. Children’s Data

Our driving lessons are available to individuals aged 17 and over (the legal age for holding a provisional driving licence in the UK). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 17.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated “last updated” date.
