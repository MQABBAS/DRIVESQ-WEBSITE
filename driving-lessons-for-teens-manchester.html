Your Teen Learning Journey

Here is what a typical learning path looks like for a 17 year old starting from scratch with DriveSQ. Every stage is tailored to your individual progress.

Stage 1: Getting Started

Cockpit Drill & Basic Controls

Your instructor introduces the car controls, mirrors, seat adjustment, and safety checks. You practise moving off and stopping on a quiet road. By the end of your first lesson, you will have driven the car yourself on a real street. This stage builds the foundation of all your future driving.

Stage 2: Building Core Skills

Steering, Gears & Road Positioning

Over the next few lessons, you develop smooth steering, learn gear changes (manual) or speed management (automatic), and practise correct positioning on different road types. You begin navigating simple junctions and left and right turns with growing independence.

Stage 3: Growing Confidence

Roundabouts, Junctions & Busier Roads

Now you tackle roundabouts, crossroads, and T-junctions in real traffic. Your instructor gradually introduces busier roads as your observation skills sharpen. You start developing the ability to read traffic flow and make your own decisions behind the wheel.

Stage 4: Advanced Skills

Manoeuvres, Dual Carriageways & Independent Driving

Master parallel parking, bay parking, and pulling up on the right. Practise dual carriageway merging and higher-speed driving. Begin independent driving using sat-nav directions, building the self-reliance you will need on test day and beyond.

Stage 5: Test Preparation

Mock Tests & Final Refinement

Complete full mock driving tests on actual test centre routes. Your instructor provides detailed feedback on any remaining weaknesses. You refine your technique until every element meets test standard. Walk into your practical test feeling genuinely prepared and confident.

How to Get Your Provisional Licence

Before your first lesson, you need a provisional driving licence. Here is exactly how to get one sorted before your 17th birthday:

  • Apply online at GOV.UK from age 15 years 9 months
  • You will need your National Insurance number
  • Have a valid passport or identity document ready
  • Costs £34 online (£43 by post)
  • Processing takes 1 to 3 weeks typically

Apply early so your licence arrives in time for your 17th birthday. You cannot take a lesson without it.

Theory Test Preparation for Teens

You must pass the theory test before booking your practical. Start revising early alongside your driving lessons to stay on track:

  • Free DriveSQ student app with DVSA-style questions
  • Hazard perception video clips included
  • Book your theory test at GOV.UK when ready
  • Theory test costs £23 at any DVSA centre
  • Your instructor can advise on theory readiness

Most teens who revise consistently for 4 to 6 weeks pass the theory test first time. Start early and stay consistent.
