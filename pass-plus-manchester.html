What Is the Pass Plus Course?

Pass Plus is a practical driving course designed by the DVSA (Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency) for drivers who have recently passed their driving test. It takes you beyond the basics of the driving test and into real-world driving situations that many new drivers find challenging or intimidating.

The course consists of six modules that cover the types of driving you are least likely to have experienced as a learner: motorways, dual carriageways, night driving, driving in all weather conditions, rural roads, and complex town driving. Each module builds your skills and confidence in a structured, supportive environment with a DVSA-approved instructor beside you.

Unlike your driving test, there is no exam at the end of Pass Plus. Your instructor continuously assesses your competence throughout each module. Once you have demonstrated the required standard across all six modules, you receive a DVSA Pass Plus certificate — a nationally recognised qualification that many insurance companies reward with significant premium discounts.

Why New Drivers Need Pass Plus

Passing your driving test proves you can drive safely in controlled conditions. But the reality of driving on your own — merging onto the M60 at rush hour, navigating country lanes at night, or handling aquaplaning in heavy rain — is a different challenge entirely. New drivers are statistically the most at-risk group on UK roads, and the first year after passing is the most dangerous.

Pass Plus addresses this head-on. By giving you structured, professional exposure to these challenging situations while an experienced instructor is beside you, the course dramatically reduces the risks you face as a new driver. It is the single most effective thing you can do to become a safer, more confident driver after passing your test.

The 6 Modules

What Pass Plus Covers

Each module is assessed by your instructor during the session — no separate test

Module 1: Town Driving

Advanced town driving skills beyond test standard. Navigating busy junctions, complex roundabouts, one-way systems, and heavy traffic in Manchester city centre. Includes dealing with buses, trams, and cyclists.

Module 2: All-Weather Driving

Driving safely in rain, fog, low sun, and poor visibility. Learn how to adjust speed, increase following distances, use fog lights correctly, and handle aquaplaning. Manchester’s weather makes this module especially practical.

Module 3: Night Driving

Driving after dark on lit and unlit roads. Learn to judge speed and distance at night, use headlights correctly, deal with oncoming glare, and navigate junctions with reduced visibility. Sessions scheduled for real darkness.

Module 4: Dual Carriageways

Joining, driving on, and leaving dual carriageways safely. Covers the A56, A57, and other Manchester dual carriageways. Learn overtaking, lane discipline, reading road signs at speed, and handling slip roads.

Module 5: Motorways

The module most new drivers need. Join the M60, M62, and M56 with your instructor beside you. Learn motorway joining and leaving, lane discipline, smart motorway rules, emergency stops, and maintaining safe gaps at 70mph.

Module 6: Rural Roads

Driving on narrow country lanes, single-track roads, and roads without markings. Learn to read the road ahead, handle blind bends, manage passing places, and deal with unexpected hazards like animals and farm vehicles.

Is Pass Plus Right for You?

Who Should Take the Pass Plus Course

Pass Plus was designed for newly qualified drivers, but it is valuable for anyone who wants to build confidence on the road. Here are the groups who benefit most from the course:

  • Newly passed drivers — If you have just passed your test and feel nervous about driving alone, especially on motorways or at night, Pass Plus gives you the structured experience you need.
  • Young drivers facing high insurance — If you are 17–25 and paying over £1,000/year for insurance, the Pass Plus discount can save you hundreds. The course often pays for itself within months.
  • Returning drivers — If you passed your test years ago but have not driven much since, Pass Plus is an excellent refresher that covers the situations you are most likely to find daunting.
  • Drivers new to UK roads — If you hold a foreign licence and have recently moved to Manchester, Pass Plus helps you adapt to UK motorways, roundabouts, and driving conditions.
  • Anyone avoiding motorways — If you have been driving but actively avoiding the M60 or M62 out of anxiety, the motorway module alone makes the course worthwhile.

You do not need to have just passed your test. Anyone with a full UK driving licence (or a valid licence that allows you to drive in the UK) can enrol in Pass Plus at any time.
