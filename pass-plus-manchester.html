Curriculum

What Pass Plus Covers

Pass Plus is a DVSA-designed course consisting of six practical modules. Each module builds your skills in a different driving environment, giving you the experience and confidence that the standard driving test simply cannot cover.

Town Driving
Navigate busy Manchester city centre traffic with confidence. Learn advanced techniques for handling complex junctions, multi-lane roundabouts, bus lanes, tram crossings, and the unpredictable stop-start flow of urban driving. We cover areas like Deansgate, Oxford Road, and the Northern Quarter where traffic density is highest and hazard perception is critical. You will practise safe positioning, effective mirror use at speed, and how to anticipate the behaviour of cyclists, pedestrians, and delivery vehicles in tight urban spaces.
All-Weather Driving
Manchester weather is famously unpredictable, which makes this module especially relevant. Learn how to drive safely in heavy rain, fog, high winds, and low winter sun. You will understand how wet roads affect braking distances, why aquaplaning happens and how to react, and how to adjust your speed and following distance when visibility drops. We also cover proper use of fog lights, demisters, and how to manage glare from wet road surfaces at night.
Night Driving
Driving after dark presents unique challenges that many newly qualified drivers find daunting. This module covers correct use of dipped and full-beam headlights, how to avoid dazzling other drivers, judging speed and distance in reduced visibility, and spotting pedestrians wearing dark clothing. We schedule evening sessions through areas like Fallowfield, Didsbury, and the A6 corridor so you experience real night driving conditions on roads you are likely to use regularly.
Dual Carriageways
Dual carriageways are faster and busier than the roads you drove on during your test. This module teaches you how to join and leave dual carriageways safely, maintain appropriate lane discipline, overtake slower vehicles confidently, and read overhead gantry signs. We use routes along the A56 Chester Road, A57 Hyde Road, and Princess Parkway — all common Manchester dual carriageways where traffic moves at 50–70 mph and lane changes require decisive action.
Motorway Driving
Since 2018, learner drivers have been allowed on motorways with an instructor, but many still pass their test without any motorway experience. This module takes you onto the M60, M62, and M56 — three of the busiest motorways in the North West. You will learn how to use slip roads to merge safely at speed, maintain a two-second gap in heavy traffic, use smart motorway variable speed limits, and handle the stress of multi-lane driving at 70 mph. We also cover breakdowns and what to do on a hard shoulder or in an emergency refuge area.
Rural Roads
Country roads account for a disproportionate number of fatal accidents among young drivers. This module takes you out of the city to roads around Glossop, Lymm, and the Cheshire countryside. You will learn how to handle narrow lanes with limited visibility, sharp bends, uneven surfaces, and unexpected hazards like tractors, horses, and cyclists. We practise reading the road ahead, adjusting speed for blind corners, and safe overtaking on single-carriageway roads where oncoming traffic is a constant factor.
Advantages

Why Take Pass Plus?

Pass Plus is not just another driving course — it is a genuine investment in your safety and your wallet. Here is why hundreds of Manchester drivers choose to complete it every year.

Insurance Discounts of 25–30%
Many major UK insurers recognise Pass Plus and offer discounts ranging from 10% to 30% on your first-year premium. For a newly qualified driver paying £1,500 or more per year, that can mean savings of £375–£450 — far more than the cost of the course. Insurers who participate include Admiral, Direct Line, Aviva, and many others. You receive a DVSA certificate as proof of completion, which you can present when getting quotes online or over the phone.
Genuine Confidence Building
The standard driving test takes place on relatively quiet roads during the day. It does not include motorways, and it rarely involves heavy rain or darkness. Pass Plus fills those gaps systematically. After completing the course, you will feel genuinely prepared for the situations that catch new drivers off guard — joining the M60 at rush hour, driving home from work in December darkness, or navigating a narrow country lane in the Peak District.
Fills Gaps in the Standard Test
The practical driving test is designed to confirm you meet the minimum safe standard. It does not — and cannot — test you in every scenario you will face as a driver. Pass Plus was created by the DVSA specifically to address this. Each of the six modules targets a driving situation that is either not included in the test or is only briefly touched upon. The result is a more complete, more capable driver.
Reduce Your Accident Risk
New drivers are statistically the most at-risk group on UK roads. One in five newly qualified drivers has an accident within their first year. Pass Plus directly addresses the most common causes — inexperience with motorways, poor hazard perception at night, and overconfidence on rural roads. By gaining structured experience in these areas with a qualified instructor beside you, you significantly reduce your likelihood of being involved in a serious incident.
