Skills We Teach in Stockport Town Centre

Road Positioning

Every right turn across oncoming traffic on the A6 (Wellington Road) demands a gap assessment. In Stockport Town Centre, DriveSQ teaches the "three-second rule" — if the approaching vehicle reaches you in under three seconds, wait. This judgement develops through guided practice at progressively busier junctions.

Reading the Road

Night driving through Stockport Town Centre introduces glare from oncoming headlights on Mersey Way. DriveSQ teaches you to look at the left kerb line rather than directly at approaching lights — maintaining your night vision while keeping directional awareness.

Vehicle Control

Reversing around corners builds spatial awareness that transfers to every other manoeuvre. On quiet Stockport Town Centre side streets, DriveSQ teaches you to use the kerb in your left mirror as your guide — keeping a consistent distance through the curve. Observation checks happen before and during every reversal.

Mock Tests

Independent driving makes up 20 minutes of your test from Bredbury. You follow either a sat-nav or road signs. DriveSQ practises both methods on Stockport Town Centre roads — the sat-nav with voice guidance, and sign-following along routes the examiner actually uses.
