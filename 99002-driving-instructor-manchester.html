What to Look for in a Manchester Driving Instructor

Before you book with any driving school, ask these questions:

  1. Are all instructors DVSA-approved with a green ADI badge? Some schools use trainee instructors (pink badge) who are still learning to teach. DriveSQ: every instructor holds a green badge.
  2. What is the first-time pass rate? Many schools will not answer this. DriveSQ: above 90%, nearly double the 47% national average.
  3. Do they charge extra for automatic? Most do. DriveSQ: same price for both.
  4. Do they use structured lesson plans or just drive around? DriveSQ: every lesson has a clear objective with progress tracking.
  5. Can you see verified reviews? DriveSQ: 100+ five-star reviews on Google, written by real learners.

How DriveSQ Instructors Teach

Our instructors do not just sit in the passenger seat and say "turn left here". They use a structured methodology built on decades of DVSA best practice:

  • Explain — every new skill is explained clearly before you attempt it, using simple language and visual references
  • Demonstrate — where helpful, your instructor shows you the correct technique before you try
  • Practice — you practise the skill in a controlled environment, with dual controls available if needed
  • Apply — you use the skill in real traffic situations, building genuine road confidence
  • Review — at the end of every lesson, your instructor gives specific, actionable feedback

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a good driving instructor in Manchester?
Check their DVSA approval (green ADI badge), read verified Google reviews, ask about their first-time pass rate, and confirm their pricing structure. DriveSQ scores highly on all four criteria.
Can I switch instructors if I am not happy?
With DriveSQ, yes. If your instructor is not the right fit, we will reassign you to another instructor in your area with no fee or awkwardness.
Do you have female driving instructors?
Yes. Female instructors are available across Manchester. Let us know your preference when booking and we will match you accordingly.
What is the difference between a green and pink badge instructor?
A green badge means fully qualified and DVSA-approved. A pink badge means the instructor is still in training — a learner teaching a learner. DriveSQ only uses green badge instructors.
