Show Me Tell Me — All Questions Answered

The complete list of 19 show-me tell-me vehicle safety questions for your 2026 driving test, with clear answers and practical tips. The easiest marks on your test — prepare these and eliminate one potential driving fault entirely.

Tell-Me Questions (Before Driving)

Your examiner will ask one tell-me question before you begin driving. You answer verbally — no physical demonstration required. These questions test your knowledge of basic vehicle safety checks.

Tyre Safety

Q: Tell me how you would check that the tyres have sufficient tread depth and are in good condition. A: The legal minimum tread depth is 1.6mm across the central three-quarters of the tyre, around the entire circumference. Check for cuts, bulges, and uneven wear. Use a 20p coin — if the outer rim is visible, tread may be insufficient.

Brakes

Q: Tell me how you would check the brakes are working before starting a journey. A: The brake pedal should feel firm and not spongy when pressed. The car should not pull to one side when braking. Test them at low speed as you set off — they should stop the car promptly without unusual noise or vibration.

Headlights

Q: Tell me how you would check that the headlights and tail lights are working. A: Turn on the ignition, switch on the headlights, and walk around the vehicle to check that both headlights and both tail lights are illuminated. Check indicators and brake lights with the help of a reflective surface or another person.

Engine Coolant

Q: Tell me how you would check the engine has sufficient coolant. A: Open the bonnet and locate the coolant reservoir (usually a transparent plastic container). Check the coolant level is between the minimum and maximum markers. Only check when the engine is cold — hot coolant is under pressure and can scald.

Show-Me Questions (While Driving)

Your examiner will ask one show-me question during the drive. You must physically demonstrate the action while maintaining safe vehicle control. Keep the demonstration brief and return both hands to the steering wheel promptly.

Windscreen Washers

Q: When it is safe to do so, show me how you would wash and clean the front windscreen. A: Operate the windscreen washer stalk (usually by pushing or pulling it) while driving. Brief operation, then return your hand to the wheel.

Horn

Q: When it is safe to do so, show me how you would sound the horn. A: Press the horn button on the steering wheel briefly. Remember: do not use the horn while stationary (unless a moving vehicle poses danger) or in a built-up area between 11:30pm and 7:00am.

Demister

Q: When it is safe to do so, show me how you would demist the front windscreen. A: Turn on the blower/fan, direct air to the windscreen, and adjust temperature to warm. Some vehicles have a dedicated demist button.

Rear Demister

Q: When it is safe to do so, show me how you would demist the rear windscreen. A: Press the heated rear window button (usually marked with a rectangle with wavy lines). This activates the heating element embedded in the rear glass.

Preparation Tip: Practise all show-me questions in your DriveSQ instruction car during your final preparation lessons. Knowing exactly where each control is located in the car you will use for your test eliminates fumbling during the examination.

Scoring Impact

Getting one or both questions wrong results in a single driving fault. This is the lowest possible penalty and will not cause test failure on its own. However, when combined with other driving faults in the same competency area, it could contribute to a total that reaches the failure threshold of 16 driving faults or a pattern-based serious fault.

The practical advice: spend 15 minutes learning all 19 answers. This minimal investment eliminates a guaranteed driving fault and demonstrates the vehicle awareness that examiners view favourably throughout the rest of your test.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many show-me tell-me questions will I be asked?
Two questions total: one tell-me question (asked before you start driving) and one show-me question (asked while driving). Getting one or both wrong counts as a single driving fault — not a serious fault and not a test failure.
What is the difference between show-me and tell-me?
Tell-me questions require you to EXPLAIN how you would carry out a safety check (verbal answer only). Show-me questions require you to DEMONSTRATE a check while driving (physical action, such as operating the windscreen washers).
Can I fail my test just from these questions?
No. Even if you get both questions wrong, you receive only one driving fault. However, if your show-me answer while driving creates a safety issue (for example, swerving while operating a control), that could be marked as a separate driving or serious fault.
Are the questions the same every time?
The examiner selects from 19 possible questions (12 tell-me, 7 show-me). You cannot predict which two will be asked, so you must prepare all 19. DriveSQ practises these questions during your final preparation lessons.
Should I worry about these questions?
They should be the least stressful part of your test. The questions are published, the answers are straightforward, and even incorrect answers carry minimal penalty. Five minutes of memorisation effort eliminates this concern entirely.
