Motorway Skills

Manchester's Motorway Network

Greater Manchester has one of the busiest motorway networks in the UK. The M60 orbital motorway carries over 180,000 vehicles daily at its busiest points. The M62 connects Manchester to Leeds and Liverpool. The M56 runs south to the airport and Cheshire. From Stretford, you will likely use these motorways regularly.

Since June 2018, learner drivers can legally take motorway lessons with an ADI in a dual-control car. DriveSQ is one of the few Manchester schools actively offering this. Most learners who skip motorway practice before their test find motorways intimidating for months afterwards.

Manchester Motorways You Will Master

M60 Orbital
Manchester's ring road. 36 miles circling the city. Junctions 1-27 including the notoriously busy Stockport and Trafford sections. Variable speed limits and smart motorway technology.
M62 Trans-Pennine
Connects Manchester to Yorkshire. Climbs to 1,221ft at junction 22 — the highest motorway point in England. Heavy HGV traffic and weather exposure on the Pennine section.
M56 Airport Route
Manchester Airport and Cheshire. Transitions from 3-lane motorway to dual carriageway. The junction 3-5 stretch has complex slip road arrangements.
M602 City Approach
Short but busy motorway connecting the M62 to Salford and the city centre. Requires confident lane selection and quick decision-making at speed.

Essential Motorway Skills

  • Joining: Building speed on the slip road to match motorway traffic flow
  • Lane discipline: Keeping left unless overtaking (the M60 has 4 lanes in places)
  • Overtaking safely: Mirror-signal-manoeuvre at 60-70mph
  • Smart motorway rules: Variable speed limits, red X compliance, emergency refuge areas
  • Exiting: Timing your lane change and deceleration on the slip road
  • Breakdowns: What to do if your car breaks down on a smart motorway with no hard shoulder
Smart Motorway Safety

The M60 around Manchester operates as a smart motorway with no permanent hard shoulder. If you break down, head for an emergency refuge area (orange SOS signs). Never stop in a live lane. Red X means the lane is closed — you must move out of it immediately. DriveSQ teaches you exactly how to handle these situations.
