Road Signs & Markings — Understanding UK Road Signs

Road signs and markings give you essential information about speed limits, hazards, directions, and restrictions. Reading them correctly is crucial for safe driving and test success.

Step-by-Step

The Road Signs & Markings Routine

1
Circles give orders: red border = prohibition (no entry, speed limit), blue = instruction (turn left)
2
Triangles warn: always red-bordered, telling you of a hazard ahead
3
Rectangles inform: blue on motorways, green on primary routes, white on local roads
4
Road markings: white dashed = lane divider, double yellow = no parking, zigzag = no stopping near crossings
5
Speed limit signs: the number in the red circle is the MAXIMUM, not a target

When to Use Road Signs & Markings

Road Signs & Markings is applied to: all roads, speed limits, junctions, lane markings, parking restrictions.

On a typical Manchester lesson, you will use Road Signs & Markings dozens of times. Every junction on Stockport Road, every roundabout on Kingsway, every lane change on Princess Parkway — Road Signs & Markings applies to all of them.
