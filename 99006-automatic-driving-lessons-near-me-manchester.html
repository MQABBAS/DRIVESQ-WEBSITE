Why Automatic Is Becoming the Smart Choice

In 2024, over 50% of new cars sold in the UK were automatic or electric (all electric cars are automatic). By 2035, all new cars must be zero-emission — meaning automatic only. Choosing automatic today is not just easier; it is future-proof.

Benefits of learning automatic

  • No clutch anxiety — the number one stress for learners is eliminated entirely
  • No stalling — impossible in an automatic. No embarrassing stops at busy junctions
  • Faster to learn — most automatic learners need 10-20% fewer hours than manual
  • Focus on the road — with no gears to think about, all your attention goes to hazards, mirrors, and positioning
  • Better for nervous drivers — less to go wrong means more confidence from lesson one
  • Perfect for city driving — Manchester's stop-start traffic is stress-free in automatic

The Automatic Licence Question

The only trade-off: passing in an automatic gives you a Category B Auto licence, which means you can drive automatic vehicles only. To drive manual, you would need to pass a separate manual test. But consider this: if your next car will be electric, hybrid, or modern automatic — which is increasingly likely — a manual licence offers no practical advantage.

The future is automatic. 100% of electric vehicles are automatic. The UK government mandates all new cars will be zero-emission by 2035. An automatic licence will cover every new car on sale.
