Intensive Driving Courses in Altrincham

What Is an Intensive Driving Course?

An intensive driving course — sometimes called a crash course — concentrates your lessons into a short period, typically one to four weeks. Instead of one lesson per week over several months, you take multiple lessons per week or even daily sessions. This immersive approach helps many learners retain skills faster and reach test standard sooner. DriveSQ intensive courses are fully personalised — we assess your starting level and build a programme that gets you test-ready efficiently.

Who Are Intensive Courses Best For?

  • Complete beginners who want to pass quickly for work or university
  • Learners who have had some lessons and want to finish their training fast
  • People relocating to Manchester who need to drive for their new job
  • Students with holiday time who want to use the break productively
  • Anyone who has failed a test and wants focused preparation for a retake
  • Licence holders from abroad who need to convert to a UK licence

Course Structures

DriveSQ offers flexible intensive formats: a 10-hour starter course (ideal if you have some experience), a 20-hour standard course (suits most beginners), a 30-hour comprehensive course (for complete beginners wanting full preparation), and a 40-hour complete course (beginners who want extra mock test practice). Sessions are typically 2-3 hours each, scheduled across consecutive days. Your instructor will confirm the right package after an initial assessment drive.

How the Course Works

Day one begins with an assessment drive to establish your current level. Your instructor then creates a structured plan covering all test-relevant skills in a logical sequence. Early sessions cover vehicle control, junctions, and roundabouts. Middle sessions introduce complex junctions, dual carriageways, and independent driving. Final sessions focus on manoeuvres, mock tests, and examiner-route practice. We book your test date to align with your course completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I learn to drive in one week?

It depends on your starting level. Learners with some experience can become test-ready in one week of intensive lessons. Complete beginners typically need 2-4 weeks.

Are intensive courses effective?

Yes. Research shows that concentrated practice helps skill retention. DriveSQ intensive learners often pass with fewer total hours than weekly learners.

How many hours per day?

Typically 2-3 hours per session. Longer sessions lead to fatigue which reduces learning quality, so we cap daily driving time.

Do you help book the driving test?

Yes. We can assist with test booking and will schedule your course to align with your test date for optimal preparation.
