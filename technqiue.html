Our Signature Driving Techniques

At DriveSQ — Manchester's top-rated DVSA-approved driving school — we teach two structured, proven driving techniques: ASTRA and SUJA. These methods form the foundation of every lesson we deliver across Greater Manchester, from Didsbury and Chorlton to Stockport, Sale, Failsworth, and Cheetham Hill. Learners who master ASTRA and SUJA not only pass their DVSA driving test — they drive safely and confidently for life.

ASTRA
The ASTRA Technique
Assess · Signal · Take Position · Reduce Speed · Act
DriveSQ's structured approach to safe junction and hazard management in Manchester

The ASTRA driving technique is DriveSQ's step-by-step method for approaching junctions, roundabouts, and road hazards safely and systematically. Taught exclusively by DriveSQ driving instructors across Greater Manchester, ASTRA gives learner drivers a clear, memorable framework that removes uncertainty and builds confidence. Whether you're learning to drive in Didsbury, Stockport, Sale, Chorlton, Levenshulme or Failsworth, ASTRA is at the core of your DriveSQ lessons.

A
A — Assess
The very first step in the ASTRA driving technique is to Assess the road situation ahead. Before doing anything else, your DriveSQ instructor will teach you to build a full mental picture of the hazard, junction, or road feature you are approaching. This means looking for road markings, traffic signs, road layout changes, the speed and position of other vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists, and any hidden dangers such as parked cars or blind bends. In the context of driving lessons in Manchester, Assessment is particularly critical because Greater Manchester's road network features complex junctions, busy roundabouts, narrow side streets, and unpredictable traffic — especially in areas like Manchester city centre, Chorlton, Rusholme, Levenshulme, and Cheetham Hill. A thorough Assessment allows you to plan your response before you need to react, reducing risk and building the kind of deep hazard awareness that DVSA examiners look for on your driving test. At DriveSQ, every lesson reinforces the habit of constant Assessment — not just at junctions but throughout every moment of driving.
S
S — Signal
Once you have Assessed the situation, the second step in the ASTRA technique is to Signal — communicating your intentions to every other road user around you. Signalling correctly and in good time is one of the most frequently tested skills in the DVSA practical driving test. DriveSQ instructors across Manchester teach you not only when to signal, but how long in advance to signal, how to assess whether a signal is even needed, and how to avoid causing confusion with poorly timed or unnecessary signals. In busy Manchester areas such as Stockport, Fallowfield, Didsbury, Wythenshawe, and Salford, signalling correctly is absolutely essential — these roads are shared by a high volume of drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians who all rely on clear communication. Your DriveSQ instructor will ensure that Signalling becomes natural and instinctive, helping you avoid the kind of signalling errors that commonly cause driving test failures across Manchester test centres.
T
T — Take Position
The third step of the ASTRA driving technique is to Take Position — moving your vehicle to the correct lane or road position for the manoeuvre ahead. Correct road positioning is one of the most important foundations of safe driving and a major component of what DVSA examiners assess during your practical test. DriveSQ instructors teaching in Manchester, Didsbury, Chorlton, Sale, Stockport, and across all Greater Manchester postcodes will help you understand exactly where your vehicle should be on the road at every moment — whether you are approaching a left turn, right turn, roundabout, pedestrian crossing, or any other feature. Poor road positioning is one of the most common reasons learner drivers fail their driving test in Manchester. The ASTRA technique solves this problem by making Taking Position a deliberate, conscious action rather than an afterthought. Over time, with DriveSQ's structured teaching, correct positioning becomes completely automatic.
R
R — Reduce Speed
The fourth step of the ASTRA technique is to Reduce Speed appropriately for the hazard or junction ahead. Speed management is central to safe driving and is assessed in detail by DVSA examiners. At DriveSQ, your Manchester driving instructor will teach you how to use progressive, smooth braking to reduce your speed before a hazard — not during or after it. This means selecting the correct gear to match your reduced speed, ensuring your vehicle is travelling at an appropriate pace to give you full control and time to make safe decisions. In Greater Manchester's busy urban environment — including routes through Ancoats, Hulme, Failsworth, Gorton, Reddish, Heaton Moor, and Urmston — speed management is particularly challenging because hazards can arise suddenly. The ASTRA technique's Reduce Speed step ensures you are always travelling at a speed that gives you the ability to Act safely, whatever the situation.
A
A — Act
The fifth and final step of the ASTRA driving technique is to Act — completing the manoeuvre, emerging from the junction, or navigating the hazard safely and decisively. After correctly Assessing the situation, Signalling your intention, Taking Position, and Reducing Speed, you are now in full control and ready to Act with confidence. Acting in the context of ASTRA means making a safe, well-timed decision — whether that is emerging from a junction, completing a turn, accelerating away, or giving way. DriveSQ instructors teach their Manchester learner drivers that Acting is never a rushed decision; it is the natural, calm conclusion of the four steps that came before it. When you consistently apply ASTRA throughout your driving lessons in Manchester, Acting becomes second nature. This is precisely why DriveSQ learners achieve a first-time pass rate of over 90% — far above the national average of 46%.

SUJA
The SUJA Technique
DriveSQ's structured observation and decision-making framework
Teaching safe, confident driving across all of Greater Manchester

The SUJA driving technique is DriveSQ's proprietary framework for structured observation, situational awareness, and safe decision-making behind the wheel. While the ASTRA technique focuses on junction and hazard management, SUJA governs how DriveSQ learner drivers process information, maintain awareness, and make confident, safe driving decisions throughout their entire journey. Together, ASTRA and SUJA form a complete driving method — unique to DriveSQ and taught to learners across Manchester, Didsbury, Chorlton, Sale, Stockport, Failsworth, Cheetham Hill, Levenshulme, Rusholme, Withington, and all Greater Manchester.

S
S — See
The first principle of the SUJA technique is See — the deliberate, systematic scanning of the full driving environment. DriveSQ instructors across Manchester teach learner drivers to develop exceptional visual awareness, looking not just directly ahead but into the near distance, to the sides, and through mirrors. The See principle of SUJA is about training your eyes and brain to gather the maximum amount of information from the road environment before that information becomes urgent. In the densely populated streets of Greater Manchester — from the busy roads of Rusholme and Levenshulme to the faster routes along the A34 through Didsbury and Withington, or the complex roundabouts of Stockport and Failsworth — the ability to See clearly and systematically makes the difference between proactive, safe driving and reactive, dangerous driving. DriveSQ's SUJA method trains the See habit through consistent repetition across every lesson, every drive, and every manoeuvre.
U
U — Understand
The second principle of SUJA is Understand — processing the visual information gathered during the See stage and turning it into meaningful awareness. Seeing is not enough on its own; a safe driver must Understand what they are seeing. Is that gap in traffic safe to enter? Is that pedestrian about to step off the kerb? Is that vehicle ahead about to brake? Is that road surface wet enough to extend braking distance? DriveSQ instructors teaching across all of Greater Manchester — including Sale, Altrincham, Stretford, Urmston, Wythenshawe, and Trafford — use the SUJA technique's Understand stage to develop their learners' ability to read the road intelligently. This is the stage that DVSA examiners assess when they look for "appropriate response to hazards" and "awareness and planning" — two of the most important competencies measured on the practical driving test. Understanding what you see transforms raw observation into genuine hazard awareness.
J
J — Judge
The third principle of the SUJA driving technique is Judge — making a calm, accurate assessment of risk and choosing the most appropriate course of action. Once you have Seen the road environment and Understood what it means, the SUJA technique teaches you to Judge: is it safe to proceed? What speed is appropriate? Should I give way? Can I safely overtake? Is there enough time to turn? The Judge step is where experience, skill, and training all come together. DriveSQ's Manchester instructors work tirelessly with their learners to develop good Judgement through structured, progressive lessons that build confidence gradually. Teaching across Didsbury, Chorlton, Cheetham Hill, Bolton, Bury, Oldham, Rochdale, and Wigan, DriveSQ instructors expose learners to the full variety of road conditions in Greater Manchester — ensuring their Judgement is tested, refined, and strengthened across every type of road and driving situation before test day arrives.
A
A — Act
The fourth and final principle of the SUJA technique is Act — executing the driving decision you have reached through See, Understand, and Judge. Just as in the ASTRA technique, Acting in SUJA is the controlled, confident conclusion of the process. When your Act is the result of proper Seeing, Understanding, and Judging, it is invariably safe, smooth, and appropriate. DriveSQ learner drivers across Manchester are taught to Act without hesitation — because hesitation at junctions, roundabouts, and crossings is itself a hazard. The goal of the SUJA Act stage is decisiveness that is born from confidence — the same confidence that comes from having correctly Seen, Understood, and Judged the situation. This complete, end-to-end process is what DriveSQ instructors across all of Greater Manchester reinforce throughout every single lesson, making SUJA a foundational habit long before test day.
