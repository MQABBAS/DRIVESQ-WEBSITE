Bredbury Driving Test Centre — Complete Guide

Test Centre Details

Bredbury Driving Test Centre is on George Lane, Bredbury, Stockport SK6 1DL. It serves Bredbury, Romiley, Marple, Woodley, and parts of Stockport and Denton. The centre is in a quieter suburban area compared to inner-city centres.

Current Pass Rate

Bredbury has a pass rate of approximately 50-53%, one of the higher rates in Greater Manchester. The suburban roads are generally quieter than inner-city centres, though they include hills, narrow lanes, and some challenging junctions. DriveSQ learners regularly achieve 90%+ at Bredbury.

Common Test Routes

Routes typically use George Lane, Stockport Road, Ashton Road, and residential areas around Romiley and Woodley. Expect the Bredbury roundabout on the A560, residential streets with speed humps, approaches to the M60 junction, and semi-rural roads towards Marple. Some routes include the Peak Forest Canal area with narrow bridges.

Difficult Junctions and Hazards

  • A560 Bredbury roundabout — multi-lane with fast-moving traffic
  • Stockport Road / Ashton Road junction — complex traffic light sequence
  • Romiley residential hills — steep gradients with parked cars
  • Narrow bridges near Marple — priority situations requiring patience
  • M60 junction approach roundabout — heavy traffic with lane discipline required
  • George Lane speed changes — transitions between 30 and 40mph zones

Tips from DriveSQ Instructors

  • Bredbury is one of the friendlier centres — but do not be complacent
  • Practise the A560 roundabout until lane selection is automatic
  • Hills in Romiley require confident hill starts — practise both methods
  • Narrow bridges test your priority awareness — know who has right of way
  • The M60 approach roundabout can be busy — stay calm and pick your gap
  • Speed limit changes are subtle — watch for repeater signs on lamp posts

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bredbury an easy test centre?

It has one of the higher pass rates in the area at 50-53%. The suburban setting is less stressful than city centres, but routes still include challenging hills, roundabouts, and dual carriageway approaches.

What is the pass rate at Bredbury?

Approximately 50-53%, above the national average. DriveSQ learners achieve 90%+ with proper route preparation.

Does the Bredbury test include dual carriageways?

Yes. Some routes approach the M60 junction and use the A560, both of which include dual carriageway sections requiring confident lane discipline.
