How to Find Driving Lessons Near You

Finding the right driving school in Manchester can feel overwhelming — there are hundreds of instructors across Greater Manchester. Here is a step-by-step guide to finding the best lessons near your location:

  1. Check your postcode coverage: Not all schools cover all areas. DriveSQ operates across every Greater Manchester postcode listed above, so you are covered wherever you are.
  2. Look for door-to-door pickup: The best schools pick you up from home, work, or university. This saves you travel time and means your lesson starts from roads you actually drive on. DriveSQ offers free door-to-door pickup on every lesson.
  3. Verify DVSA approval: Every DriveSQ instructor holds a green DVSA badge, meaning they have passed the official three-part qualifying exam. Avoid schools that use trainee instructors with pink badges for your main lessons.
  4. Ask about local test centre knowledge: Your instructor should know the routes at your nearest test centre inside out. DriveSQ instructors practise test routes at all six Manchester test centres as part of every course.
  5. Read recent Google reviews: Look for reviews from learners in your specific area. DriveSQ maintains a 4.9-star Google rating with reviews from learners in every part of Manchester.
  6. Compare total cost, not hourly rate: A cheaper hourly rate often means more hours needed. DriveSQ learners average 35-45 hours to become test-ready, compared to the national average of 47 hours.
