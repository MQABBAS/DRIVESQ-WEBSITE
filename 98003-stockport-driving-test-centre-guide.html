Stockport Driving Test Centre — Complete Guide

Test Centre Details

Stockport Driving Test Centre is located on Dialstone Lane, Stockport SK2 6QU. It serves Stockport, Hazel Grove, Bramhall, Cheadle, Marple, and Reddish. The centre is in a residential area with on-street parking.

Current Pass Rate

Stockport has a pass rate of approximately 44-47%. Routes include challenging hills, narrow residential streets, and busy A-roads. The hilly terrain adds complexity that flat-area learners may not expect. DriveSQ learners achieve 86%+ at Stockport.

Common Test Routes

Routes typically use Dialstone Lane, the A6 Wellington Road, Buxton Road towards Hazel Grove, and residential areas around Great Moor and Davenport. Expect steep hills on approaches to Offerton and Marple Bridge, the A6 dual carriageway, and the Stockport town centre gyratory system.

Difficult Junctions and Hazards

  • A6 Wellington Road — fast dual carriageway with lane changes approaching roundabouts
  • Stockport town centre gyratory — complex one-way system with multiple exits
  • Dialstone Lane hills — steep gradients requiring confident hill starts
  • Buxton Road / Bramhall Lane junction — busy crossroads with filter lights
  • Great Moor residential streets — narrow with cars parked on both sides
  • Railway bridges near Davenport — single-file priority situations

Hill Start Preparation

Stockport is one of the hillier test centres in Greater Manchester. You must be confident with hill starts using both handbrake and clutch control. Practise on Dialstone Lane itself and the steep roads around Offerton. Your instructor should ensure you can hold the car on a gradient, find the biting point smoothly, and move off without rolling back.

Tips from DriveSQ Instructors

  • Hill starts are almost guaranteed — practise until they are second nature
  • The A6 requires confident speed — do not drive too slowly on the dual carriageway
  • Watch for cyclists on Buxton Road — it is a popular commuter cycle route
  • The gyratory in town can feel overwhelming — practise it multiple times
  • Residential streets around Great Moor have many 20mph zones — check every sign
  • Use engine braking on steep descents rather than riding the brake

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Stockport a hard test centre?

It is considered moderately difficult due to hilly terrain and the town centre gyratory. With proper preparation, DriveSQ learners consistently pass at high rates.

Will I need to do hill starts at Stockport?

Almost certainly. The area around Dialstone Lane is hilly. Practise hill starts with both handbrake and clutch-only methods.

What is the pass rate at Stockport?

Approximately 44-47%. DriveSQ learners typically achieve 86%+ through focused route preparation and hill-start practice.

Stockport Local Area Guide

Lessons around Stockport use real local roads including Princes Street, Higher Hillgate and Greek Street, so by the time you're ready for your test you've already driven the streets you'll use every day after passing. Stockport's Market Place sits on a 240-million-year-old sandstone cliff above the River Mersey, and the town was granted its Market Charter around 1220, making it one of the oldest market towns in the North West.

We also plan around school-run traffic near Werneth School and Stockport Academy, using quieter spots like Stockport Market Place for early manoeuvre practice before stepping up to busier sections of Princes Street.

Test centre: most learners around Stockport test at Bredbury Driving Test Centre, Lingard Lane, Bredbury, Stockport SK6 2QT; mock tests are planned around the routes examiners actually use from there.
