Pass Your Test in 1–4 Weeks

Need to pass your driving test quickly? DriveSQ's intensive crash courses in Manchester compress weeks or months of learning into a focused 1–4 week programme of daily lessons. Whether you have a job starting, university approaching, or simply want your licence as fast as possible, our structured intensive courses deliver results without cutting corners.

How Intensive Courses Work

An intensive course replaces the traditional once-or-twice-a-week lesson format with daily sessions of 2–3 hours. This immersive approach keeps your skills fresh between sessions, reduces the time spent re-learning from the previous week, and allows you to build momentum rapidly. You will typically drive every weekday, with weekends off to rest and process what you have learned.

DriveSQ intensive courses follow the same structured lesson plan as our regular lessons — the same skills, the same standards, the same test preparation — just compressed into a shorter timeframe.

Typical Intensive Course Timelines

  • 1-week intensive (10–15 hours) — ideal for learners who already have some experience or have been practising privately. Focuses on polishing skills, manoeuvres, and mock tests. Suits returning drivers or those who had lessons previously but did not complete their test.
  • 2-week intensive (20–25 hours) — the most popular choice. Suitable for learners with basic skills who need to reach test standard quickly. Covers intermediate skills, manoeuvres, independent driving, and multiple mock tests.
  • 3-week intensive (30–35 hours) — ideal for near-complete beginners who learn quickly. Covers the full syllabus from cockpit drill to test-day preparation.
  • 4-week intensive (35–45 hours) — the most comprehensive option for complete beginners. Allows time for thorough practice of all skills, extensive route knowledge, and multiple full mock tests.

A Typical Day on an Intensive Course

Your daily schedule on a DriveSQ intensive course might look like this:

  • Session 1 (2 hours, morning) — skill-building or route practice. Your instructor covers new skills or reinforces areas from the previous day.
  • Break (30–60 minutes) — rest is essential during intensive learning. Your instructor will drop you at home or a nearby cafe while you take a proper break.
  • Session 2 (1–1.5 hours, afternoon) — consolidation drive. Practising what you learned in the morning on different roads and in different traffic conditions.

We limit daily driving to 3–4 hours maximum because concentration fades beyond this point. More hours does not mean faster progress — quality practice with proper rest is far more effective than exhausting marathon sessions.

Who Suits an Intensive Course?

Intensive courses work best for learners who:

  • Can dedicate 1–4 weeks of free time (no work or study commitments during the course)
  • Handle pressure well and enjoy immersive learning
  • Are motivated and have a clear deadline (job, university, move)
  • Can absorb information quickly without becoming overwhelmed

They are not ideal for very nervous learners or those who need more time to process new skills. If you are anxious about driving, our regular twice-weekly lessons may be a better fit — there is no shame in taking a steadier approach.
