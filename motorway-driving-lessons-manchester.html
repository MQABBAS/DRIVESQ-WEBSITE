Motorway Driving Lessons in Wilmslow

Manchester's Motorway Network

Greater Manchester is encircled by the M60 orbital motorway and connected to the rest of the UK by the M62 (east-west), M56 (south), M61 (northwest), and M66 (north). After passing your test, you will use these roads almost daily for commuting, shopping trips, and travel. DriveSQ ensures you are ready from day one.

What You Will Learn

Joining the Motorway

The slip road is your acceleration zone. DriveSQ teaches progressive gear changes through the slip road, building speed to match lane-one traffic before the merge point. The critical principle: merge at traffic speed, not below it. Entering a 70mph motorway at 40mph creates a dangerous speed differential.

Lane Discipline

Lane one is the default driving lane. You move to lane two or three only to overtake, then return to lane one. DriveSQ drills this pattern because "middle-lane hogging" is both illegal (since 2013) and a common test fail point for instructors assessing post-test readiness.

Smart Motorway Protocols

The M60 around Manchester operates as a smart motorway. Variable speed limits displayed on overhead gantries are legally enforceable — cameras monitor compliance. Red X signs mean the lane is closed and must not be entered under any circumstances. DriveSQ teaches you to scan gantries continuously and respond promptly to changing signals.

Emergency Procedures

Breakdowns, tyre blowouts, and debris require specific motorway responses. DriveSQ covers emergency refuge area locations on the M60, the procedure for reaching the hard shoulder (where available), and the protocol for calling Highways England if stranded in a live lane.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can learners drive on motorways?
Yes, since June 2018. Learner drivers can use motorways when accompanied by a DVSA-approved instructor in a dual-controlled car. DriveSQ offers dedicated motorway lessons across the M60, M62, and M56.
Which Manchester motorways do you teach on?
The M60 (orbital), M62 (east-west), M56 (south to airport), M602 (Eccles link), and M67 (Hyde). Each motorway presents different challenges: slip road lengths, HGV density, and traffic volumes vary significantly.
Is motorway driving part of the driving test?
No. The standard practical test does not include motorway driving. However, motorway skills are essential for independent driving after passing. DriveSQ includes motorway preparation as part of comprehensive training.
What are smart motorways?
Smart motorways use technology to manage traffic: variable speed limits displayed on overhead gantries, hard shoulder running during busy periods, and red X lane closures. The M60 around Manchester operates as a smart motorway. DriveSQ teaches full smart motorway protocols.
