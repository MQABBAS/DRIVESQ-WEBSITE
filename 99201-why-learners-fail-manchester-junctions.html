INSIDER INTEL — THE 12 KILLER JUNCTIONS

The 12 Manchester Junctions Where Learners Fail Their Test

Every Manchester driving instructor knows them. The specific junctions, roundabouts, and merges that claim the most test failures. This page names them, explains exactly what goes wrong, and tells you how to handle each one. No other driving school publishes this information.

1. Queens Road / A665 Roundabout (Cheetham Hill)

Why learners fail here

Five exits, multiple lanes, and fast-moving traffic. Learners either choose the wrong lane (because signs are 200m back and easy to miss), or attempt a dangerous last-second lane change. The examiner expects you to be in the correct lane BEFORE you reach the roundabout.

Fix: Know the lane markings for your exit before you arrive. If you miss your lane, go around again — it is a minor fault, not a serious one. A dangerous lane change IS a serious fault.

2. Palatine Road / Barlow Moor Road Junction (West Didsbury)

Why learners fail here

Complex traffic light sequence with filter arrows. Learners either enter the junction on amber (running a red), or position too far left/right for their intended direction. The left-turn filter catches people who do not notice it.

Fix: Approach in the correct lane for your direction. Watch for filter arrows — green filter means your direction is protected, but still check for pedestrians crossing.

3. A56 Washway Road Lane Changes (Sale)

Why learners fail here

The A56 through Sale requires multiple lane changes in quick succession. Learners forget mirror checks in the rush, or signal too late and cut across traffic. The examiner watches mirror use on EVERY lane change.

Fix: Plan your lane changes early. Mirror-signal-position well before you need to move. If you miss a lane change opportunity, wait for the next safe gap — do not force it.

4. Dialstone Lane Hill Start (Stockport)

Why learners fail here

The test often begins with a hill start on Dialstone Lane itself. Learners who are nervous stall, roll back, or over-rev. Starting the test with a serious fault destroys confidence for the rest of the drive.

Fix: Practise hill starts on Dialstone Lane until they are completely automatic. Use the handbrake method for reliability. Extra gas, clutch to biting point, release handbrake. No rolling.

5. M67 Junction Roundabout (Hyde)

Why learners fail here

Motorway slip road traffic merging with local traffic creates a fast, complex roundabout. Learners hesitate too long (blocking traffic) or pull out into insufficient gaps. The speed difference between motorway traffic and local traffic is the killer.

Fix: Approach at an appropriate speed — not too slow. Match your gap judgement to the speed of the traffic. Commit when a gap appears. Hesitation at busy roundabouts is as dangerous as rushing.

6. A560 Bredbury Roundabout

Why learners fail here

Multi-lane approach with confusing road markings. Learners end up in the wrong lane and either straddle the line (serious fault) or make an unsafe lane change.

Fix: Read the road markings 100m before the roundabout. Follow lane arrows exactly. If wrong lane, follow where your lane takes you and recalculate.

7-12. More Killer Junctions

  • 7. Princess Parkway slip roads — short merge lanes requiring quick acceleration. Learners join too slowly.
  • 8. Mancunian Way curves — elevated dual carriageway with no hard shoulder. Speed + tight curves = anxiety.
  • 9. Stockport town gyratory — multi-lane one-way system with exits appearing quickly.
  • 10. Wilmslow Road / Palatine Road mini-roundabout — learners approach too fast and fail to give way.
  • 11. Sale one-way system entries — wrong lane selection with no room to correct.
  • 12. Gee Cross hills (Hyde) — steep residential roads with parked cars creating single-track situations.
