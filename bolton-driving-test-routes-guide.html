About This Centre

About Bolton Test Centre

Bolton Test Centre is located at Bridgeman Place, Bolton, BL2 1BT. With an average pass rate of approximately 45%, it sits slightly below the national average of 49.3%. The centre serves learners from Farnworth, Horwich, Westhoughton, Little Lever and surrounding areas.

DriveSQ instructors use Bolton test routes in every lesson for local learners. By the time you reach your test date, you will have driven every route the examiner might choose — multiple times. This familiarity is a key reason our learners achieve pass rates nearly double the Bolton average.

The test lasts approximately 40 minutes and covers around 6-8 miles. You will complete an eyesight check, two show-me-tell-me questions, approximately 20 minutes of independent driving following a sat-nav, and one reversing manoeuvre (parallel park, bay park, or pull up on the right).

DriveSQ Tip
Book your test for a Tuesday or Wednesday morning if possible — pass rates tend to be higher mid-week when roads are quieter. Avoid Friday afternoons and school run times (3-4pm).
Test Routes

Common Bolton Test Routes

These are the most frequently used routes from Bolton Test Centre. Your DriveSQ instructor will ensure you have driven each one before your test day.

Chorley New Road Route
West along the A673 toward Horwich. This route includes the Beehive Roundabout, residential streets in Lostock, and dual carriageway sections. Examiners test your confidence at higher speeds and your lane discipline on the approach to the Middlebrook retail area.
Crompton Way Route
North on the A666 through Astley Bridge. Features the busy Dunscar roundabout, 30mph residential zones, and steep hills around Eagley. Tests your hill start ability and speed management on the descent back toward the town centre.
St Peters Way Route
Through Bolton town centre via the ring road. Multiple traffic light-controlled junctions, bus lanes (watch the operating hours), and pedestrian crossings near the bus station. Requires confident lane changes and awareness of road markings.
Beaumont Road / Wigan Road Route
South-west toward Deane. Beaumont Road is wide but has a 30mph limit that catches many learners. The Daubhill roundabout is busy, and residential streets off Wigan Road have cars parked heavily on both sides.
Moss Bank Way Route
North-east toward Sharples. Moss Bank Way (B6536) has 40mph and 30mph sections — watch for the speed limit changes. The route passes Moss Bank Park where pedestrians cross regularly, and includes narrow streets in Halliwell.
Tricky Spots

Tricky Spots & Expert Tips

These are the junctions and roads where Bolton test candidates most commonly pick up faults. Master these and you are well on your way to passing.

This large multi-lane roundabout in the town centre handles heavy traffic. You must choose your lane early based on your exit. Mirror-signal-position well in advance. The road markings guide you — trust them and don't panic if traffic is heavy.

The Blackburn Road / Astley Bridge junction has multiple traffic light phases. Watch for the filter arrow — many learners sit waiting at a green filter without moving. Also check for cyclists in the advanced stop line area.

Several roads through the centre have bus lanes with time restrictions. If the bus lane is in operation, you must not drive in it. If it's outside operating hours, you can use it. Read the signs carefully — examiners will note any bus lane infringement.
