Pass Plus Course in Marple

You passed your test — congratulations. Now develop the skills your test did not cover. DriveSQ's Pass Plus course tackles motorways, night driving, adverse weather, and high-speed roads across Greater Manchester.

Beyond the Driving Test

The practical driving test assesses your ability to drive safely in standard daytime conditions on urban and suburban roads. It does not test motorway driving, night driving, or driving in heavy rain, fog, or ice. These are precisely the conditions that cause the most accidents among newly qualified drivers. Pass Plus fills that gap.

The Six Modules

Town Driving

Advanced urban techniques: complex junctions, bus lanes, tram crossings, one-way systems. Practised in Manchester city centre.

All-Weather Driving

Wet road techniques, aquaplaning awareness, fog protocols, ice and frost management. Manchester provides ample real-world weather practice.

Out of Town

Rural and semi-rural roads: bends, gradients, farm vehicles, reduced visibility. Practised on roads towards the Peak District.

Night Driving

Headlight use, dazzle management, reduced visibility assessment, pedestrian detection in darkness. Evening sessions on Manchester roads.

Dual Carriageways

A-road driving at national speed limits: overtaking, lane discipline, slip road technique. A56, A34, A6 practice routes.

Motorways

M60, M62, M56 driving: joining, lane changing, smart motorways, emergency procedures. The module most new drivers value highest.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pass Plus?
Pass Plus is a DVSA-designed course for newly qualified drivers covering six modules: town driving, all-weather driving, driving out of town, night driving, dual carriageways, and motorways. It builds skills that the standard test does not assess.
How long is the Pass Plus course?
Pass Plus requires a minimum of six hours of practical driving, typically delivered across 2-3 sessions. DriveSQ covers all six modules on Manchester roads over 2-3 lessons of 2-3 hours each.
Will Pass Plus reduce my insurance?
Many insurers offer discounts of 5-15% for Pass Plus certificate holders. The discount varies by insurer and your individual circumstances. DriveSQ provides a list of insurers known to offer Pass Plus discounts.
When should I do Pass Plus after passing my test?
Ideally within 6-12 months of passing. Your driving skills are sharpest immediately after test preparation, making Pass Plus modules easier to absorb. DriveSQ recommends booking within three months of passing.
Does DriveSQ issue the Pass Plus certificate?
Yes. DriveSQ instructors are DVSA-registered Pass Plus trainers. Upon completing all six modules to the required standard, we issue the official DVSA Pass Plus certificate.
