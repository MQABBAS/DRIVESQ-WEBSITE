What Is Pass Plus?

Pass Plus is a DVSA-designed practical training course that builds on the skills you developed before your driving test. It covers six real-world modules that your standard lessons may not have fully addressed.

Module 1: Town Driving

Sharpen your skills in busy urban environments. Handle complex junctions, pedestrian crossings, bus lanes, and congested city centre traffic with greater awareness and composure.

Module 2: All-Weather Driving

Learn to adapt your driving for rain, fog, ice, and strong winds. Understand how stopping distances change, when to reduce speed, and how to maintain full control in poor conditions.

Module 3: Rural Roads

Navigate narrow country lanes, unmarked junctions, blind bends, and roads shared with cyclists, horse riders, and agricultural vehicles. Build judgement for roads without clear markings.

Module 4: Night Driving

Gain confidence driving after dark. Practise correct use of headlights, dealing with glare from oncoming vehicles, judging distances at night, and navigating unlit roads safely.

Module 5: Dual Carriageways

Master higher-speed merging, safe overtaking, lane discipline, and reading road signs at speed. Build the confidence to handle slip roads and multi-lane traffic flow without hesitation.

Module 6: Motorways

Experience real motorway driving with a qualified instructor beside you. Practise joining via slip roads, maintaining safe following distances at 70 mph, overtaking lorries, and exiting smoothly.

Course Module Breakdown

Each module is delivered by your DVSA approved instructor in a structured, progressive order. Here is what to expect during your advanced driving course.

Module 1

Motorway Driving

You will join the motorway from a slip road, practise maintaining a safe following distance at national speed limit, change lanes with confidence, and learn to read overhead gantry signs. Your instructor covers smart motorway rules including red X compliance and variable speed limits.

Module 2

Night Driving

Experience driving in darkness across urban and rural roads. You will learn when to use dipped and full beam headlights, how to cope with dazzle from oncoming traffic, and how to judge speed and distance when visibility is reduced. Particular focus is given to spotting pedestrians and cyclists at night.

Module 3

Adverse Weather Conditions

Your instructor covers driving techniques for heavy rain, fog, and icy conditions. You will understand aquaplaning, how to adjust braking distances, correct use of fog lights, and how wind affects vehicle stability, particularly on exposed stretches of motorway and dual carriageway.

Module 4

Rural & Country Roads

Navigate single-track lanes, blind corners, and roads with limited passing places. You will learn to anticipate hazards around bends, deal with oncoming traffic on narrow roads, and adjust speed for surfaces that may be loose or uneven.

Module 5

Dual Carriageways

Build on the skills from your standard lessons with focused higher-speed driving. Practise safe overtaking of slower vehicles, reading ahead for lane reductions, and using slip roads efficiently. This module bridges the gap between test-standard driving and everyday road use.

Module 6

Town Centre Driving

Refine your urban driving through Manchester's busiest areas. Handle bus gates, tram crossings, complex signal-controlled junctions, and multi-lane roundabouts. Your instructor will fine-tune your observation and positioning in high-traffic scenarios you encounter daily.
