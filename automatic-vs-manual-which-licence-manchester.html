The Big Decision

More than 40% of UK driving tests are now taken in automatic cars — and the number is rising every year. In Manchester, where stop-start traffic on roads like Wilmslow Road, the A6, and Princess Road is constant, an automatic gearbox removes a major source of stress. But a manual licence gives you more flexibility. Let's break it down.

Automatic Licence: Pros & Cons


✅ Advantages


  • Easier to learn — focus on the road, not the gears

  • Typically fewer lessons needed (save money)

  • Less stressful in Manchester's heavy traffic

  • No stalling at junctions (huge confidence boost)

  • Modern cars are increasingly automatic/electric

  • Same price as manual at DriveSQ (£35/hr)


❌ Limitations


  • You can only drive automatics (not manuals)

  • Fewer second-hand options (though this is changing)

  • Some employers (delivery, fleet) prefer manual licences

Manual Licence: Pros & Cons


✅ Advantages


  • Drive ANY car — automatic or manual

  • More second-hand options when buying

  • Better resale value in some markets

  • Some driving jobs require manual

  • Greater control on steep Manchester hills (Stockport, Bolton, Oldham)


❌ Limitations


  • More lessons typically needed (higher total cost)

  • Clutch control adds stress for nervous learners

  • Stalling risk at busy junctions (embarrassing, not dangerous)

  • More multitasking in heavy traffic

DriveSQ's Recommendation
