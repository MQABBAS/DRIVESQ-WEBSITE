What to Expect on Your First Driving Lesson

Before Your First Lesson

Your first lesson should feel exciting, not scary. Before your lesson: make sure you have your provisional driving licence, wear comfortable shoes with thin soles (no chunky trainers or heels), wear comfortable clothing that does not restrict movement, eat a light meal, and have water available. Your DriveSQ instructor will pick you up from your door — be ready 5 minutes before the scheduled time.

Meeting Your Instructor

Your instructor will introduce themselves, check your provisional licence, and have a brief chat about your goals. They will ask about any previous driving experience (including off-road, abroad, or in a car park with family). They will explain how the lesson works, how the dual controls work (they can brake and steer if needed), and what you will cover today. This chat takes about 10 minutes and sets the tone for a relaxed, supportive relationship.

What You Will Learn on Day One

  • Cockpit drill — adjusting your seat, mirrors, headrest, and seatbelt for safe driving position
  • Dashboard controls — handbrake, indicators, wipers, lights, and demister
  • Starting and stopping the engine
  • Clutch control (manual) or creep control (automatic) — finding the biting point
  • Moving off slowly in a safe, quiet location
  • Stopping smoothly using brake and clutch coordination
  • Basic steering on a straight, quiet road

Where the First Lesson Happens

DriveSQ first lessons take place in quiet, low-traffic areas near your home. Your instructor will drive you to a suitable location — typically a quiet industrial estate, residential cul-de-sac, or empty car park. You will not be on busy roads on day one. The environment is controlled and calm, giving you space to make mistakes safely.

After Your First Lesson

Your instructor will give you honest feedback on how you did (spoiler: everyone is nervous, and everyone does better than they expected). They will outline what you will cover next lesson and suggest any practice or theory study. You will agree a schedule for future lessons. Many learners leave their first lesson surprised at how much they achieved in just 60-90 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need my provisional licence for my first lesson?

Yes, absolutely. Your instructor must see your valid provisional driving licence before you can drive. Without it, the lesson cannot go ahead.

Will I drive on roads on my first lesson?

Usually not on busy roads. Your first lesson takes place in a quiet, low-traffic area where you can learn basic controls without pressure.

What shoes should I wear?

Flat shoes with thin soles that allow you to feel the pedals. Avoid chunky trainers, heels, boots, or sandals.

How long is the first lesson?

We recommend 90 minutes for your first lesson — this gives time for the introduction, learning basic controls, and some initial driving practice.
