Understanding Crash Courses

What Is a Crash Course?

A crash course is the fastest route to your full driving licence. Instead of spreading lessons over months, you take concentrated, back-to-back sessions over a short period — typically 1 to 5 days or 1 to 2 weeks — so that everything stays fresh in your mind.

Unlike traditional weekly lessons where you forget half of what you learned between sessions, a crash course builds momentum. You practise daily, your muscle memory develops quickly, and you reach test standard far sooner.

Crash Course vs. Regular Intensive Course

While both terms are used interchangeably, a crash course usually refers to the most compressed option — 4 to 6 hours per day over a very short timeframe. A standard intensive driving course might spread the same hours over 3 to 4 weeks. At DriveSQ, we offer both and tailor the schedule around you.

Your Daily Schedule

What to Expect on a Crash Course Day

Every day of your crash course is carefully structured to maximise learning while keeping you fresh and focused. Here's what a typical day looks like.

  • Free pick-up from your home, work, or university
  • 4-6 hours of tuition per day (with breaks)
  • Morning theory, afternoon practical focus
  • Access to our free student learning app
  • Drop-off back to your chosen location
  • Progress review and next-day planning
Morning Session (9:00 - 12:00)

Start with new skills and techniques while your concentration is at its peak. Your instructor introduces manoeuvres, junctions, or roundabouts and you practise on quieter roads first before moving to busier routes.

Lunch Break (12:00 - 13:00)

A proper rest is essential. We drop you home or to a nearby cafe. This downtime lets your brain process what you've learned and you return refreshed for the afternoon.

Afternoon Session (13:00 - 16:00)

Time to consolidate. You revisit the morning's skills with added complexity — heavier traffic, dual carriageways, independent driving. By the end of day, those new skills feel natural.

End of Day Review

Your instructor gives honest, constructive feedback on your progress. You'll know exactly where you stand and what tomorrow's focus will be. No guesswork, no surprises.
