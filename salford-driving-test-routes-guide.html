Test Routes

Common Salford (Worsley) Test Routes

These are the most frequently used routes from Salford (Worsley) Test Centre. Your DriveSQ instructor will ensure you have driven each one before your test day.

Walkden Road / East Lancashire Road Route
North from the test centre along Walkden Road toward the East Lancashire Road (A580). The A580 is a 40-50mph dual carriageway with multiple roundabouts. Tests your speed management, lane discipline, and ability to safely join and leave a fast-moving road.
Worsley Road / Worsley Brow Route
South toward Worsley village. Worsley Brow is a steep, winding descent past the Bridgewater Canal — many learners find this challenging. The village has narrow roads, parked cars, and a tricky mini-roundabout at the bottom. Tests your clutch control and road positioning.
Bolton Road / Little Hulton Route
North-west through Little Hulton. Bolton Road has 30mph and 40mph sections with schools nearby. Residential streets in Little Hulton have heavy parking — you'll practise squeezing through gaps and deciding who has priority. The Kenyon Way roundabout is large and busy.
Manchester Road / Swinton Route
East toward Swinton town centre. Manchester Road passes through Pendlebury with its mix of shops and residential areas. The Swinton Church junction is complex with multiple exits. You'll also navigate Chorley Road with its bus lanes and 20mph school zones.
Leigh Road / Boothstown Route
West toward Boothstown and Astley. Leigh Road has long straight sections at 40mph, residential areas at 30mph, and the Boothstown roundabout. The route includes quiet country-like roads around Astley where you might encounter horse riders and agricultural vehicles.
