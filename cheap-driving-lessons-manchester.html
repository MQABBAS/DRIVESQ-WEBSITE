Hidden Costs to Watch For When Choosing a Driving School

Before signing up with any Manchester driving school, ask about these commonly hidden fees:

  1. Automatic surcharge: Many schools charge £2-5 extra per hour for automatic lessons. Over 40 hours, that is £80-200 extra. DriveSQ charges the same for auto and manual.
  2. Fuel surcharge: Some independent instructors add £3-5 per lesson for fuel, especially since petrol prices rose. This is never mentioned upfront. DriveSQ: no fuel surcharge, ever.
  3. Short-notice cancellation fees: Budget schools often charge the full lesson fee if you cancel with less than 48 or even 72 hours notice. Life happens — DriveSQ only requires 24 hours notice for free cancellation.
  4. Test day lesson charge: Some schools charge double for the lesson on your test day because it includes driving to the test centre and waiting. DriveSQ charges standard rate for test day.
  5. Admin or booking fees: A £20-50 "registration fee" or "admin charge" that appears when you sign up. DriveSQ has no admin fees or registration charges.
  6. Mandatory block purchases: Some cheap schools require you to buy a 10 or 20-hour block upfront — no pay-as-you-go option. DriveSQ offers flexible pay-as-you-go at £35/hr with no minimum commitment.
  7. Non-refundable deposits: Avoid schools that take large non-refundable deposits. DriveSQ block bookings are fully refundable for unused lessons.
