Driving in Ancoats — What You Need to Know

Ancoats sits at the north-eastern edge of Manchester city centre, bordered by Great Ancoats Street to the west, Oldham Road (A62) to the north, and the Ashton Canal to the east. This location means you encounter some of the busiest junctions in the city during every lesson, including the major signals at the junction of Great Ancoats Street and Oldham Road, and the multi-lane approach to the inner ring road at Pin Mill Brow.

Key Roads and Junctions

Great Ancoats Street (A665) is one of the most important roads you will learn. It carries heavy traffic in both directions and features multiple traffic-light junctions, dedicated cycle lanes, and bus gates that can catch out unprepared drivers. Pollard Street connects Ancoats to the Etihad Campus and Beswick, requiring confident lane positioning through staggered crossroads. Every Junction Road and Bengal Street provide practice with narrow residential streets and parked-car hazards.

Navigating the Northern Quarter Edge

Lessons starting in Ancoats naturally extend into the Northern Quarter via Oldham Street, Stevenson Square, and Lever Street. These streets teach you to deal with high pedestrian footfall, delivery vehicles blocking lanes, and taxi traffic. Our instructors use these routes to build your situational awareness — exactly the skill the examiner is assessing.

Test Centre Preparation

Ancoats learners typically test at Cheetham Hill, which is a short drive via Rochdale Road (A664) or Queens Road. Our instructors know every Cheetham Hill test route in detail, including the tricky right turn from Queens Road onto Broughton Lane and the residential streets around Cheetham Park. We also offer preparation for West Didsbury test centre if you prefer a suburban route.
