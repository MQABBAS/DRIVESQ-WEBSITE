What Is a 5 Day Intensive Driving Course?

Everything you need to know before you book.

A 5-day intensive driving course packs weeks of regular driving lessons into a single, focused week. Instead of one or two lessons per week spread over months, you train for approximately 6 hours every day — split into a morning and afternoon session — with the same DVSA approved instructor throughout.

At DriveSQ, your week ends with a practical driving test, meaning you could hold a full UK driving licence by Friday afternoon. This format is ideal for learners who need their licence quickly for work, university, or life changes.

Unlike other schools that rush you through with unqualified instructors, every DriveSQ instructor is fully DVSA approved and experienced in intensive course delivery. We also provide a free student learning app so you can revise theory and hazard perception between sessions.

Converting a foreign licence or returning to driving after years away? An intensive refresher can rebuild your confidence and road skills quickly.

Before You Start

Make sure you have these sorted before your course begins.

Valid Provisional Driving Licence

You must have a valid UK provisional licence. If you do not have one, apply on the DVLA website — it takes about a week to arrive.

Theory Test Certificate

You need a valid theory test pass to book a practical test. We recommend passing your theory before starting the intensive course. Our free learning app can help you prepare.

Eyesight Requirements

You must be able to read a number plate from 20 metres. If you wear glasses or contact lenses, bring them to every lesson.

A Clear Week

Block out 5 full days with no other commitments. You will be driving for around 6 hours a day — it is tiring but hugely rewarding.

Preparation Tips for Success

A little preparation before your course starts can make a huge difference to your outcome.

Pass Your Theory First

Do not leave your theory test until the last minute. Pass it before your intensive course begins so your practical test can be booked for the end of the week. Use our free learning app to prepare.

Get Plenty of Sleep

Six hours of driving per day is mentally and physically demanding. Go to bed early each night, eat well, and stay hydrated throughout the week.

Clear Your Schedule

Treat this week like a full-time job. Cancel social plans, take time off work, and give yourself the best chance of absorbing everything your instructor teaches you.

Wear Comfortable Shoes

You will be using the pedals for hours. Wear flat, thin-soled shoes that let you feel the pedal pressure — avoid thick trainers, heels, or heavy boots.

Study Between Sessions

Use your lunch break and evenings to review what you learned. Watch YouTube videos of manoeuvres, study road signs, and go through the DriveSQ learning app.

Stay Positive & Patient

There will be tough moments — stalling at junctions, misjudging roundabouts, nerves on busy roads. This is completely normal. Trust the process and trust your instructor.

Ask Questions

Your instructor is there to help. If something does not make sense, ask immediately. The more you communicate, the faster you will progress.
