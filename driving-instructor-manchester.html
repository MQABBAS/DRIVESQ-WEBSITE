DVSA Approval

What Does DVSA-Approved Actually Mean?

When you are searching for a driving instructor in Manchester, one of the first things you should check is whether they are DVSA-approved. The Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) is the government body responsible for setting and maintaining driving standards across the UK. An Approved Driving Instructor (ADI) has undergone one of the most rigorous qualification processes in the professional teaching world.

To become a DVSA-approved driving instructor, a candidate must pass three separate examinations:

  • Part 1 — Theory and Hazard Perception Test: A comprehensive multiple-choice exam and hazard perception assessment testing the instructor’s road knowledge, traffic law, and ability to identify developing hazards.
  • Part 2 — Driving Ability Test: An advanced driving test lasting approximately one hour, where the candidate must demonstrate an exceptionally high standard of driving far beyond what is expected of a normal driving test candidate.
  • Part 3 — Instructional Ability Test: A practical teaching assessment where the candidate must deliver a driving lesson to a DVSA examiner who role-plays as a learner. This tests the instructor’s ability to communicate, adapt to the learner’s needs, and provide effective feedback.

Only instructors who pass all three parts receive the green ADI badge, which must be displayed on the windscreen of the tuition vehicle at all times. Some instructors operate with a pink badge, which indicates they are still in training and have only passed parts one and two. While pink-badge instructors are legally allowed to charge for lessons, they have not yet proven their teaching ability to the DVSA.

Every DriveSQ driving instructor in Manchester holds the green ADI badge. We do not employ trainee instructors. When you book a lesson with DriveSQ, you are guaranteed a fully qualified, DVSA-assessed professional who has demonstrated excellence in both driving and teaching.

The DVSA also conducts standards checks on registered ADIs. These are periodic assessments where a DVSA examiner observes a real lesson and grades the instructor on their teaching competence. DriveSQ instructors consistently achieve Grade A (the highest possible grade) in these standards checks, reflecting our commitment to continuous professional development and teaching excellence.

Our Approach

Structured Progressive Lessons — Our Teaching Methodology

Every DriveSQ lesson follows a proven, structured approach designed to build confidence and competence systematically.

At DriveSQ, we do not believe in ad-hoc lessons where you simply drive around Manchester for an hour with no clear objective. Every lesson follows our structured progressive methodology — a carefully designed teaching framework that takes you from complete beginner to test-ready in the most efficient way possible.

Phase 1: Foundation Skills (Lessons 1–8)

Your first lessons focus on vehicle control fundamentals. You will learn cockpit drill, moving off and stopping, steering technique, gear changes (manual) or speed management (automatic), and basic road positioning. These lessons typically take place on quiet residential streets where you can build confidence without the pressure of heavy traffic. Your instructor will choose roads they know are ideal for beginners — wide, well-surfaced, and with minimal junctions.

Phase 2: Road Procedure (Lessons 9–18)

Once your vehicle control is solid, we progress to junctions, roundabouts, traffic lights, lane discipline, and dealing with other road users. Your instructor introduces increasingly complex road situations at a pace that matches your confidence. You will practise on real Manchester roads — the busy A34 Kingsway, the roundabouts along Princess Road, and the junctions around Fallowfield and Withington. Each lesson has a clear objective, and your instructor reviews your progress at the end of every session.

Phase 3: Advanced Skills (Lessons 19–28)

This phase covers manoeuvres (parallel parking, bay parking, pulling up on the right), independent driving using sat-nav, dual carriageway driving, and complex junction work. Your instructor will take you to the areas around your designated test centre — whether that is Sale, Cheetham Hill, or Failsworth — so you become familiar with the roads, junctions, and hazards that commonly appear on test routes.

Phase 4: Test Preparation (Lessons 29+)

The final phase is all about mock tests and polishing. Your instructor conducts full mock driving tests under realistic conditions, marking you using the same criteria a DVSA examiner would use. You will receive detailed feedback on every minor and serious fault, with targeted practice to eliminate recurring errors. By the time you sit your actual test, you will have completed multiple full mock tests and driven every common test route from your centre.

Manual vs Automatic

Manual or Automatic — Which Should You Choose?

One of the most common questions new learners ask their driving instructor in Manchester is whether they should learn in a manual or automatic car. It is an important decision, and at DriveSQ we help you make the right choice for your circumstances — without any price pressure, since both cost the same £33/hr.

Manual Driving Lessons

Learning to drive a manual car gives you the most flexibility. A full manual driving licence allows you to legally drive both manual and automatic vehicles. If you want to keep all your options open — for example, if you might drive a company car, hire cars abroad, or share vehicles with family members who drive manual — then a manual licence is the more versatile choice. Manual lessons involve learning clutch control, gear changes, hill starts, and engine braking in addition to all the standard road skills.

Automatic Driving Lessons

An automatic licence restricts you to driving automatic vehicles only. However, automatic cars are increasingly common — and with the growth of electric vehicles (which are all automatic), this restriction matters less every year. Many learners choose automatic because it is simpler and faster to learn. Without the need to manage a clutch and gears, you can focus entirely on road awareness, hazard perception, and driving technique. Nervous learners often find automatic lessons less stressful, and many progress to test readiness in fewer lessons than they would in a manual.

DriveSQ’s Approach

Unlike many Manchester driving schools that charge £3–£8 more per hour for automatic lessons, DriveSQ charges the same £33/hr for both. Your instructor will discuss the pros and cons of each transmission type during your first lesson and help you make an informed decision. There is no wrong answer — the best choice is the one that gets you driving confidently and safely.
