Adapted Vehicle Lessons in Manchester

Our adapted driving programme removes physical barriers so you can focus on learning to drive safely and confidently across Manchester.

Hand Controls
Push-pull or twist-grip hand controls for acceleration and braking, allowing full vehicle control without foot pedals. Fitted to our dual-controlled training vehicles.
Steering Aids
Spinner knobs, fork grips, and palm grips fitted to the steering wheel to accommodate reduced hand strength or limited grip, ensuring safe and precise steering.
Individual Assessment
Before your first lesson, we discuss your specific needs and configure the vehicle accordingly. Every adaptation is checked and tested before you start driving.
Patient Instruction
Our instructors are experienced in teaching with adapted controls. We understand that adapting to different equipment takes time and we never rush the process.
How It Works

Your Journey to Driving Independence

Initial Consultation

Before any lessons begin, we arrange a free consultation to understand your physical requirements, discuss which adaptations would work best for you, and explain the DVLA notification process. This meeting ensures that your first lesson is productive rather than administrative.

Vehicle Configuration

Based on your consultation, we fit the appropriate adaptations to your training vehicle. Whether you need hand controls, pedal modifications, steering aids, or a combination, everything is set up and tested before you arrive. We also ensure our dual controls work seamlessly alongside your adaptations for maximum safety.

Progressive Learning Plan

Your lesson plan is fully personalised. We start with familiarisation with the adapted controls in a quiet area, then progressively introduce road types and traffic conditions as your confidence and skill develop. There is no standardised timeline—you progress at exactly the pace that works for you.

Equal Access: Adapted vehicle lessons cost the same £35/hr as standard lessons. We believe driving independence should be accessible to everyone regardless of physical ability.
Available Adaptations
  • Hand controls (push-pull/twist)
  • Steering wheel spinner knobs
  • Left-foot accelerators
  • Pedal extensions
  • Fork and palm grips
  • Electronic secondary controls
DVLA Requirements

You must notify the DVLA of any medical condition that could affect your driving. Your provisional licence may include specific restriction codes for the adaptations you use.
