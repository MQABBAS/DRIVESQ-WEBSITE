Driving Lesson Options

Standard weekly lessons

60 or 90-minute sessions, once or twice per week. Best for learners who want steady progress alongside work, school, or university. Available mornings, afternoons, evenings (until 8pm), and weekends. Most learners reach test standard in 30-40 hours over 4-6 months.

Semi-intensive courses

3-4 lessons per week over 4-6 weeks. Ideal if you want to pass faster without the intensity of a full crash course. Retains the benefit of time between lessons to process what you have learned, while building skills quickly.

Intensive crash courses

Daily 2-3 hour sessions over 1-4 weeks. Best for learners with a deadline — a new job, university, or a time-limited opportunity. We offer 10, 20, 30, and 40-hour packages. Your instructor assesses you on day one and confirms which package suits your level.

Specialist lessons

  • Nervous driver programme — patient, gradual approach starting in quiet environments
  • Refresher lessons — for qualified drivers rebuilding confidence
  • Pass Plus — motorways, night driving, and adverse weather for new pass holders
  • Test preparation — mock tests on real examiner routes with fault analysis

Your First Lesson — What to Expect

Your instructor picks you up from your door. You will spend 10 minutes chatting about your goals and any previous experience. Then you will drive to a quiet location and learn the basics: seat and mirror setup, moving off, stopping, and basic steering. You will not be on busy roads. By the end of the lesson, most beginners are surprised at how much they achieved. Your instructor will outline a plan for the next few lessons and confirm your regular booking slot.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much are driving lessons in Manchester?
DriveSQ starts from £28/hr in promotional areas. Standard rate across Greater Manchester is £33/hr. All prices include pick-up, drop-off, and dual-control vehicle. No extra charge for automatic.
How long does it take to learn to drive in Manchester?
Most DriveSQ beginners reach test standard in 30-40 hours. The DVSA national average is 45 hours. Factors affecting your hours include previous experience, lesson frequency, and whether you do private practice between lessons.
Do you teach in my area?
We cover all of Greater Manchester including Didsbury, Chorlton, Stockport, Sale, Salford, Oldham, Bolton, Bury, and everywhere in between. We pick up from any address at no extra charge.
Can I learn automatic?
Yes, and we charge the same as manual. Automatic removes clutch stress and many learners pass faster. The only trade-off is a licence restricted to automatic vehicles.
What if I am very nervous?
We have specialist nervous driver instructors trained in gradual, patient teaching. We start in quiet environments and progress at your pace. Automatic is available to reduce stress further.
