Why Learn to Drive at University?

Manchester's public transport is excellent for getting to lectures, but a driving licence opens up your entire post-graduation life. Graduate jobs increasingly list "full driving licence" as essential. Clinical placements, teaching roles, engineering positions, and sales jobs all require the ability to drive. Learning while you study means your licence is ready the moment you graduate.

University also offers scheduling advantages that working life does not. You have flexible weekday hours between lectures, long summer breaks for intensive courses, and three years in one city to build driving experience on local roads.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer student discounts?
DriveSQ offers competitive block booking rates that benefit students: 10 hours for £330 (saving £20). We also offer flexible pay-as-you-go at £35/hr with no upfront commitment.
Can you pick me up from campus?
Yes. DriveSQ collects from University of Manchester (Oxford Road), Manchester Metropolitan University (All Saints), University of Salford (Crescent), and any other Manchester campus. Just share your preferred pickup point.
Can I fit lessons around lectures?
Absolutely. DriveSQ offers lessons seven days a week, 7am-8pm, including evenings and weekends. Many students book 90-minute sessions between lectures or Saturday morning slots.
Should I learn during term time or holidays?
Both work. Term-time lessons on local roads give you consistent weekly practice. Holiday intensive courses let you progress rapidly when academic demands drop. DriveSQ offers both approaches.
Which test centre is nearest to the universities?
Cheetham Hill (15 minutes from Oxford Road campus) or West Didsbury (10 minutes from Fallowfield halls). DriveSQ practises routes from both centres.
