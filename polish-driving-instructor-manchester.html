UK Driving Guide

Driving in the UK: What’s Different

Left-Hand Driving

The most fundamental difference is driving on the left side of the road. This affects everything from junction approaches to roundabout direction to overtaking. Our Polish-speaking instructor explains these changes in context, using Polish road system comparisons so the new rules click naturally rather than feeling arbitrary.

Speed Limits & Road Signs

UK speed limits use miles per hour rather than kilometres per hour. The default limits are 30mph in built-up areas, 60mph on single carriageways, and 70mph on dual carriageways and motorways. Many road signs also differ from Polish standards. We cover every sign and marking you will encounter, explained clearly in Polish.

Roundabouts & Junctions

UK roundabouts rotate clockwise (opposite to Polish convention). Priority rules, lane selection, and signalling requirements are covered methodically during your lessons. Manchester has hundreds of roundabouts from mini to multi-lane, so you get extensive practice with every type during your training.

Cultural Understanding: Our Polish-speaking instructors understand the specific challenges Polish drivers face when adapting to UK roads. This cultural awareness accelerates your learning and prevents common cross-system errors.
Key Differences
  • Drive on the left
  • Speed in mph, not km/h
  • Roundabouts go clockwise
  • Different road markings
  • Give way rules differ
  • Amber means stop (not go)
Licence Exchange

Polish EU licences are valid for driving in the UK. If you need to exchange to a UK licence, the DVLA process is straightforward and we can guide you through it in Polish.
