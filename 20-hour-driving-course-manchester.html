What's Included in Your 20 Hours

Our comprehensive beginner curriculum takes you from your very first cockpit drill right through to full mock test preparation. Every skill is taught, practised, and perfected.

Cockpit Drill & Moving Off

Start with the absolute basics. Learn how to set up your seat, mirrors, and steering wheel correctly. Master moving off safely, stopping under control, and building a solid foundation before you ever touch a main road.

Clutch Control & Gear Changes

Develop a feel for the biting point, smooth gear transitions, and hill starts without rolling back. These fundamentals are drilled until they become second nature, removing the anxiety that holds many beginners back.

Junctions & Roundabouts

Progress to navigating T-junctions, crossroads, and roundabouts of all sizes. Learn when to give way, how to position your car correctly, and how to judge gaps in traffic with growing confidence.

All Test Manoeuvres

Parallel parking, forward and reverse bay parking, and pulling up on the right with a reverse. Each manoeuvre is broken down step by step and rehearsed until you can perform it calmly under test conditions.

Dual Carriageways & Lane Discipline

Build higher-speed confidence on dual carriageways. Practise safe merging, maintaining lane position, overtaking, and reading road signs at speed so you are comfortable on faster roads after you pass.

Mock Tests & Exam Preparation

Your final hours include full-length mock driving tests on real test routes from your local centre. Your instructor acts as examiner, providing detailed feedback so you walk into your test knowing exactly what to expect.

Your Structured 20 Hour Lesson Plan

Every hour is purposefully planned. Here is a typical roadmap showing how your twenty hours unfold with DriveSQ, taking you from absolute beginner to test-ready.

Phase 1: Hours 1-4

Fundamentals & Vehicle Control

Your journey begins with the cockpit drill, understanding the controls, and learning to move off and stop safely. You will spend time on quiet residential roads mastering clutch control, smooth gear changes, and steering technique. By hour four, you will be driving on busier roads with growing assurance.

Phase 2: Hours 5-8

Road Skills & Junctions

Now that you are comfortable controlling the car, you progress to navigating junctions, T-junctions, crossroads, and mini roundabouts. You will develop your observation skills, learn the correct approach positioning, and practise emerging safely into flowing traffic across various road types.

Phase 3: Hours 9-13

Intermediate & Advanced Skills

Move on to multi-lane roundabouts, dual carriageways, complex junctions, and traffic light sequences. This phase also introduces all four test manoeuvres: parallel parking, forward and reverse bay parking, and pulling up on the right. Each is broken down and practised repeatedly.

Phase 4: Hours 14-17

Test Routes & Independent Driving

Drive the actual routes used by examiners at your local test centre. Practise following sat-nav instructions independently for extended periods, handling unexpected situations, and making safe decisions without guidance. This builds the self-reliance the examiner wants to see on test day.

Phase 5: Hours 18-20

Mock Tests & Final Preparation

Complete two to three full mock driving tests under realistic exam conditions. Your instructor provides detailed post-drive analysis, highlighting any minor or serious faults. These final sessions polish your driving to test standard and eliminate last-minute nerves so you feel fully prepared.
