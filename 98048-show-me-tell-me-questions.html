Show Me Tell Me Questions — Complete List and Answers

What Are Show Me Tell Me Questions?

At the start of your driving test, the examiner asks one tell-me question (answered verbally while stationary) and one show-me question (demonstrated while driving). Getting one or both wrong results in one minor fault — it will not fail you, but it is an easy mark to keep. There are 14 possible tell-me questions and 7 possible show-me questions.

Tell Me Questions and Answers

  • Tell me how you would check the brakes are working — Press the brake pedal firmly. It should feel firm and not spongy. The car should not pull to one side.
  • Tell me how you would check the tyres — Check tyre pressure with a gauge against the manufacturer's recommended pressure (found in the handbook or door sill). Visual check for cuts, bulges, and tread depth — minimum 1.6mm across the central three-quarters.
  • Tell me how you would check the headlights and tail lights — Turn on the ignition and headlights. Walk around the vehicle (or ask someone) to check all lights are working. Check indicators separately.
  • Tell me how you would know if the ABS warning light was working — The ABS warning light should illuminate when you turn on the ignition and go out shortly after. If it stays on, there is a fault.
  • Tell me how you would check the direction indicators — Turn on the ignition, apply the indicators, and walk around to check (or check reflections in windows/garage doors).
  • Tell me how you would check the power-assisted steering — On starting the engine, you can check by gently turning the steering wheel — it should turn easily. If it feels heavy, there may be a fault.
  • Tell me how you would check the horn — Press the horn button. It should produce a clear sound.

Show Me Questions (Demonstrated While Driving)

  • Show me how you would wash and clean the rear windscreen — Operate the rear windscreen washer (the examiner may accept you demonstrating while stationary if driving would be unsafe)
  • Show me how you would wash and clean the front windscreen — Operate the windscreen washer and wipers
  • Show me how you would set the rear demister — Press the heated rear windscreen button
  • Show me how you would switch on the dipped headlights — Operate the headlight switch to the dipped position
  • Show me how you would open and close a window — Use the electric window switch (usually on the driver's door)
  • Show me how you would demist the front windscreen — Set the blowers to windscreen, increase fan speed, and set temperature to warm. Turn on air conditioning if available
  • Show me how you would switch your headlights from dipped to main beam and back — Operate the main beam switch (usually a stalk behind the steering wheel)

Tips for Show Me Tell Me

Practise in your instructor's car so you know exactly where every control is. For show-me questions, glance briefly at the control but keep your focus on the road — the examiner is also assessing your driving while you answer. If you are unsure, give your best answer confidently — one wrong answer is only a minor fault.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many show me tell me questions are there?

There are 14 possible tell-me questions and 7 possible show-me questions. You will be asked one of each on your test.

What happens if I get show me tell me wrong?

Getting one or both wrong results in one minor fault. It will not fail your test on its own.

Should I memorise the answers?

Yes. These are easy marks. Learn the answers and practise in your instructor's car so you know where every control is.
